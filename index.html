FAQs

As the technical person you probably are as a computer science student, doing a course on writing might be quite intimidating. This FAQ page seeks to answer the grueling questions you might have to make sure you as comfortable as possible.

On Course Structure

Anticipating the differences (and similarities) between a communication class and a computer science class

Taking a professional communications course varies greatly in focus from a technical computer science couse. While computer science courses focus on understanding logic, the functional structure of systems, and overall an engineering-focused mindset, in a course like this, we are focused on interpersonal relationships with our readers and colleges. Thus, you are encouraged to think more empathetically rather than logically. Not to be too stereotypical, but as a Computer Science major, I imagine you are more inclined toward the latter rather than the former. Knowing this, you may have to push your limits a bit if you want to excel in this class. However, the one way your experience in computer science courses may translate to this course is you ability to manage complex group activities which will play a role in writing your final report.

Navigating the D2L site

The structure of the D2L page may be confusing at first compared to what you are used to in CPS courses. The main content of the D2L page you will need to access are split into these sections: the assignment guidelines and references, the weekly course content, and the course slides (see figure 1 below). The assignment guidelines and references contains the descriptions of your assignments and templates for some of them, the weekly course content navigates you to pages with the readings and quizzes for a given week, and the course slides page allows you to access the course slides for all the lectures.

On Excelling In This Course

Excelling in assignments and quizzes

To excel in the assignments and quizzes, you need to make sure you read and understand the course content. Intentionally going over the reading and internalizing the important writing advice is key to this. The quizzes specifically will test you on the knowledge of the writing technicals you learned from the readings so you may want to write notes on the concepts beforehand. As for the assignments, they are a test of your ability to apply these concepts. If you need to, practice beforehand and go into detail analyzing any examples the text gives you.

Managing Group Work Effectively

Managing group work effectively is an important aspect of the course, especially for your final report. For you final report, you will do some activities to manage your group effectively to ensure you reach your goal of writing an effective report. For this, you will develop a group contract outlining how work will be done in your group and deadlines you will follow. If you can, managing group work this way is effective in keeping everyone accountable and on the same page. Additionally, having routine meetings can help each other get over blockers and ensure effective communication between members.
